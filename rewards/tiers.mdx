XP isn't the whole story. At the end of every Season, Speculate looks at how you competed — not just how much [XP](/rewards/xp) you racked up — and hands out an **Elite tier**. Land in one, and your entire Season's XP gets multiplied before it's locked in.

## Two ways to qualify

Every tier has two doors in — you only need to walk through one:

- **Volume** — your total buy-in across the Season's competitions crosses a dollar threshold.
- **First places** — you rack up enough first-place finishes, regardless of how much you staked to get there.

Whichever condition you hit first, you're in. A grinder running small entries to a pile of wins qualifies exactly the same as a high-roller putting up huge buy-ins.

## The tiers

| Tier | Qualify (volume **or** first places) | XP bonus |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Diamond** | \$1,000,000 volume or 8 first places | **\+50%** |
| **Platinum** | \$500,000 volume or 5 first places | **\+20%** |
| **Gold** | \$250,000 volume or 3 first places | **\+15%** |
| **Silver** | \$100,000 volume or 1 first place | **\+10%** |
| **Bronze** | Compete in the Season (participation) | **\+5%** |

<Note>
  **Bronze is the floor, not a hurdle.** Every player who competes in a Season lands in Bronze (+5%) automatically — the higher tiers are what you climb toward with volume or first-place finishes.
</Note>

## The formula

Your tier bonus multiplies your full Season XP total, and the value that counts is locked in when the Season closes:

$$
\text{Final XP} = \text{Total XP} \times (1 + \text{Tier Bonus})
$$

A player who closes the Season with **10,000 XP** and qualifies for **Gold** (\+15%) locks in **11,500 XP** — no extra grinding required once the tier is earned.

<Tip>
  Tiers don't stack. Only your **highest qualifying tier** applies to the multiplier — Diamond overrides Platinum, Platinum overrides Gold, and so on down the line. Hitting the volume bar for one tier and the first-place bar for another doesn't combine them; the better of the two wins.
</Tip>
